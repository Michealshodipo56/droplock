ENCRYPTION_AND_ANONYMITY_STANDARDS

PROTOCOL_01 // ENCRYPTION

AES-256 GCM

The industry standard for authenticated encryption. Every byte of your data is locked with military-grade precision. Our Galois/Counter Mode (GCM) implementation provides not only high-speed encryption but also data integrity verification, ensuring your locker remains tamper-proof against any form of unauthorized manipulation. Even if an adversary accesses the encrypted blob, they cannot alter it without detection.

PROTOCOL_02 // KEY_DERIVATION

Argon2id

Winner of the Password Hashing Competition. DropLock utilizes Argon2id—a state-of-the-art memory-hard function—to derive encryption keys locally. By combining the strengths of Argon2i (side-channel resistance) and Argon2d (brute-force resistance), we make GPU-accelerated cracking attempts mathematically infeasible. Your locker identifier is the seed, but the key stays exclusively in your control.

PROTOCOL_03 // ANONYMITY

Tor Optimized

True privacy requires a masked origin. DropLock is specifically architected for routing through the Tor network. Our protocols minimize header overhead and eliminate persistent trackers like Fingerprinting variables or Session Cookies. Every request is scrubbed of identifying metadata, allowing you to establish a secure link from the shadows with total confidence in your untraceables.

PROTOCOL_04 // STORAGE

Zero-Knowledge

We operate on the principle of minimal trust. Our servers are designed as "blind" vaults; they store encrypted blobs but have no mechanism to decrypt them. We do not store passwords, recovery hints, or plaintext metadata. Even a total system compromise would yield nothing but static—meaning your privacy is structurally guaranteed rather than just promised.

PROTOCOL_05 // PERSISTENCE

Ephemeral Tunnels

Data existence is tied to session activity. DropLock implements an "Ephemeral Storage" model where data is only as persistent as you need it to be. Inactive lockers are automatically purged from system memory, and session data exists only within the local sandbox. There are no "Recycle Bins" or "History Logs"—once you disconnect, the tunnel collapses permanently.

PROTOCOL_06 // FUTURE_PROOF

Quantum Readiness

Privacy must survive the future. While our current AES-256 standard remains secure against classical computation, we are actively auditing post-quantum cryptographic primitives. Our architecture is designed to be "pluggable," allowing for a seamless transition as new global standards for quantum-resistant encryption emerge. Your secrets remain secure across generations.
